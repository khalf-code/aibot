Do No Harm**\n- Avoid making predictions that could cause anxiety or harm\n- Be cautious with health, financial, or relationship advice\n- Refer to appropriate professionals when beyond scope\n- Avoid exploitative or manipulative practices\n\n**2. Transparency & Honesty**\n- Clearly state beliefs are interpretive, not scientifically proven\n- Disclose limitations of predictions and readings\n- Be honest about experience and qualifications\n- Avoid claims of absolute certainty or supernatural powers\n\n**3. Informed Consent**\n- Explain what to expect from a reading before beginning\n- Allow clients to opt out of any topic or card\n- Respect client boundaries and comfort levels\n- Clear communication about session structure\n\n**4. Client Autonomy**\n- Emphasize client's free will and agency\n- Frame readings as guidance, not destiny\n- Encourage critical thinking and personal interpretation\n- Support client's own intuition and decision-making\n\n**5. Cultural Respect**\n- Acknowledge cultural origins of practices\n- Avoid cultural appropriation\n- Credit source cultures appropriately\n- Collaborate with and compensate practitioners from source cultures\n\n### Professional Boundaries\n\n**Scope of Practice:**\n\n**Appropriate Areas:**\n- Self-reflection and personal growth\n- Exploring options and perspectives\n- Timing and cyclical patterns\n- Identifying strengths and challenges\n- Narrative and storytelling\n- Spiritual guidance within beliefs\n\n**Inappropriate Areas (Refer to Professionals):**\n- Medical diagnoses or treatment\n- Legal advice\n- Financial investment advice\n- Mental health diagnosis or therapy\n- Addiction counseling\n- Crisis intervention (suicide, abuse)\n\n**Red Flags to Watch For:**\n- Client expressing thoughts of self-harm\n- Situations of abuse or violence\n- Signs of severe mental illness\n- Financial dependence on readings\n- Obsessive or compulsive behavior\n\n**Professional Practice:**\n\n1. **Session Structure**\n   -
